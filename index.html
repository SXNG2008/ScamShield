Remote Access Scam (AnyDesk/TeamViewer)

Click to expand
Avg loss: ₹3.8L
"Sir, I'm calling from SBI Fraud Department. We've detected a suspicious transaction of ₹47,000 from your account. I need to help you block it immediately — please install this app."
  • Call comes from a number spoofed to appear as the official SBI/HDFC helpline. Truecaller shows the bank name.
  • Scammer builds trust: provides victim's last 4 digits of card (bought from data brokers for ₹2 per record on dark web).
  • Creates urgency: "Your account will be permanently blocked in 90 minutes unless we verify now."
  • Victim is asked to install AnyDesk or QuickSupport from Play Store — "it's a standard bank verification tool."
  • Victim shares the 9-digit AnyDesk code displayed on screen. Scammer now has full device control.
  • Scammer opens banking app, initiates transfers to mule accounts while keeping victim distracted with conversation.
  • Even without OTP being spoken aloud — scammer sees OTP on screen and enters it on their own device simultaneously.
  • Average call duration: 23 minutes. Total loss often occurs in the last 4 minutes of that window.
Victim: All urban ages, especially 40–65 Signal: Remote app + unknown call + banking session Apps: AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport

Digital Arrest Scam

Click to expand
Avg loss: ₹12L+
"This is DCP Sharma, Central Bureau of Investigation, Delhi. Your Aadhaar number has been used to open 14 illegal bank accounts. You are being placed under digital arrest. Do not speak to anyone. Do not disconnect."
  • WhatsApp video call from a "verified" number — scammer in uniform, fake CBI badge visible, professional background.
  • AI-generated deepfake video of real senior police officers used to appear on screen for additional authenticity.
  • Victim is told they face 7–14 years imprisonment for crimes they didn't commit: drug trafficking, money laundering, POCSO.
  • Isolation weapon: "If you tell family or hang up, we will issue immediate arrest warrant. This is a national security matter."
  • Psychological torture over hours or days — victim is kept under video surveillance, forbidden from sleeping.
  • Victim told to transfer money to "RBI Safe Account" or "Enforcement Directorate escrow" to prove innocence.
  • A 92-year-old in Delhi: ₹2 crore lost. A doctor in Hyderabad: 70 hours of surveillance, cardiac arrest, death.
  • PM Modi addressed this specifically in October 2024 Mann Ki Baat: "There is no such thing as digital arrest in Indian law."
Victim: Professionals 45+, retired officials Signal: Extended video call + unknown number + UPI activity Tools: WhatsApp video + AI deepfake face/voice

AI Voice Clone Scam (Family Emergency)

Click to expand
83% victims lose money
"Maa! Main accident mein hun. Police ne pakad liya. Mujhe ₹50,000 chahiye abhi — bail ke liye. Kisi ko mat batana, main theek ho jaunga." [In your son's exact voice]
  • Scammer harvests 30–60 seconds of target's child/sibling voice from Instagram Reels, YouTube videos, WhatsApp status.
  • Open-source AI voice cloning tools (freely available) generate a real-time cloned voice in under 3 minutes.
  • Call placed to parent from unknown number — but the voice is indistinguishable from their actual child.
  • Urgency + panic overrides all rational thinking: "accident," "police," "hospital," "arrest" are maximum fear triggers.
  • 69% of Indians cannot distinguish AI-cloned voice from real human voice — confirmed by Tenable Research 2025.
  • Parent navigates to UPI app within 2 minutes of call — one of the fastest fraud-to-payment sequences recorded.
  • Money transferred to mule account. Scammer disconnects. Parent calls real child — discovers the fraud. Too late.
  • 47% of Indian adults have been directly affected by AI voice scams. 83% of those lost money. Source: ORF/McAfee 2025.
Victim: Parents 45–70, rural and urban Signal: Unknown number + saved contact mismatch + immediate UPI Tools: ElevenLabs, RVC, open-source voice models

QR Code "Scan to Receive" Scam

Click to expand
NPCI: 6.32L cases in 6 months
"I want to rent your house. I'll send the advance — ₹30,000. Just scan this QR code to receive the payment into your account. It's just for bank account verification."
  • Victim posts ad: house for rent, item for sale, freelance service, OLX/99acres/Quikr listing.
  • Scammer contacts as interested buyer, agrees instantly to terms without negotiating — red flag most miss.
  • Sends a QR code via WhatsApp: "Scan this to receive the advance payment."
  • Victim scans with UPI app — but QR is a payment REQUEST, not a receive link. UPI's UI looks identical for both.
  • Victim enters their UPI PIN believing they are "accepting" the incoming payment.
  • Money flows OUT. Scammer provides "2 rupee test" first to build trust, then sends high-value QR immediately after.
  • Key technical exploit: UPI does not clearly differentiate collect-request QR from payment QR in confirmation screen.
  • Physical variant: QR stickers replaced overnight on kirana shops, parking meters, restaurants — payments diverted.
Victim: Sellers, landlords, freelancers, shopkeepers Signal: QR scan + UPI PIN entry + call coaching simultaneously Platform: Any UPI app — PhonePe, GPay, Paytm

KYC Expiry / Fake Bank Portal Scam

Click to expand
Fastest growing — up 182% YoY
"URGENT: Your SBI KYC has expired. Your account will be suspended at midnight. Update now: [bit.ly/sbi-kyc-update]"
  • SMS sent with exact bank formatting, sometimes from spoofed sender IDs like "VM-SBIPAY" or "AD-HDFCBK".
  • Link opens a pixel-perfect clone of the bank's actual website — same logo, same colors, same layout, different URL.
  • Victim enters account number, registered mobile, Debit card number, expiry, CVV on fake site.
  • Scammer uses these credentials simultaneously on the REAL bank website — triggering an OTP to victim's phone.
  • Fake site prompts: "Enter the OTP sent to your registered number to complete KYC." Victim enters it.
  • Scammer now has the OTP — enters it on real bank site. Account access granted. Fund transfer initiated.
  • Entire process takes under 4 minutes. Victim thinks they completed KYC. Account is empty.
  • India's e-KYC boom (Jio, banks, UIDAI) has created the perfect psychological cover for this scam's legitimacy.
Victim: 30–65, any bank customer, Jio users Signal: OTP received + unknown link + banking credential entry Method: Phishing site + real-time OTP relay attack

Fake Customer Care Number Scam

Click to expand
Google SEO-driven mass targeting
"Thank you for calling Amazon Customer Care. Your order has been flagged for a refund of ₹4,299. I'll need to verify your payment details to process it."
  • Scammers purchase Google Ads or do SEO to rank fake "SBI helpline," "Amazon India customer care," "Paytm support" numbers at top of search results.
  • Victim Googles "[bank name] customer care number" — gets the scammer's number. This is not a hack. This is Google.
  • Scammer answers professionally, has fake agent name and ID, transfers calls to "senior manager" for authenticity.
  • Victim has initiated this call — they believe they called the real company. Trust is pre-built by this reversal.
  • Scammer requests remote access "to process the refund" or "verify the transaction" — leads to AnyDesk flow.
  • Alternatively: asks for OTP "sent by our system" — actually OTP from real bank for a fund transfer.
  • TRAI and MHA have issued repeated warnings. Google has been notified repeatedly. Fake numbers keep appearing.
  • This scam is uniquely dangerous: the victim was searching for help and found harm instead.
Victim: Anyone who Googles a helpline number Signal: Outbound call + remote app install + OTP during call Platform: Google SEO/Ads + VoIP phone systems
Victim Psychology

Why smart people fall for this

The victim is not stupid. The victim is not uneducated. The victim's rational brain has been deliberately, systematically disabled using five well-documented psychological weapons. This is why awareness doesn't work. This is why ScamShield must act at the device level.

01

Authority hijack

The human brain is evolutionarily wired to comply with perceived authority — police, bank, government. When someone presents with a badge number, official language, and a uniform, compliance is the default response, not questioning.

02

Manufactured urgency

"Account blocked in 2 hours." Urgency triggers the prefrontal cortex (rational decision-making) to shut down. Under genuine time pressure, humans revert to fight-or-flight. "Flight" in this context means comply and make the threat stop.

03

Fear of social shame

Accusations of drug trafficking, money laundering, POCSO — crimes the victim knows society judges harshly. The fear of family finding out, colleagues knowing, is often more powerful than the fear of financial loss itself.

04

Deliberate isolation

"This is confidential. If you tell anyone, you will be arrested immediately." Isolation removes every real-time check on the victim's reality. The one intervention that would work — a trusted person saying "this is a scam" — is explicitly blocked.

05

Incremental commitment

Small compliance first — share your name, install an app, scan a ₹2 QR. Once a person complies once, sunk-cost psychology makes them continue. "I've already come this far" overrides "this doesn't feel right."

06

Why information doesn't help

Knowledge of scams does not override the acute stress response. The same people who watched awareness videos, shared cybercrime news, told others to "be careful" — are getting scammed. The mechanism bypasses the knowledge layer entirely.

The insight that powers ScamShield

Every scam on this list — remote access, digital arrest, AI voice, QR, KYC, fake customer care — exploits the exact same psychological state: a person under live voice pressure, being walked step by step through actions on their phone, with their rational thinking deliberately suppressed. The attack method differs. The psychological state is identical. And that psychological state creates a measurable, detectable behavioral fingerprint on the victim's device.

The Solution

ScamShield — Behavioral Fraud Interruption

We are not building a scam detector. We are building a behavioral interruption system that detects the single psychological state common to every digital scam in India — a user under live voice pressure, being walked through actions on their device — and intervenes before the money moves.

The core insight: you cannot reliably detect the scammer. Their numbers change. Their voices are cloned. Their websites are pixel-perfect. But you can detect the victim being scammed — because social engineering forces the victim to behave in ways that are measurable, consistent, and anomalous across all six scam types.

1

Passive behavioral monitoring — always on, zero battery impact

ScamShield runs as an Android foreground service with minimal CPU footprint. It continuously monitors a cluster of device signals: call state, active applications, screen sharing status, incoming SMS content category, app install events, and screen interaction rate. No audio is recorded. No content is read. Only behavioral metadata — the what, not the what-is-said.

2

Rolling fraud confidence score — signal cluster detection

Within a 15-minute rolling window, ScamShield assigns a fraud confidence score based on which signals co-occur and in what sequence. No single signal triggers anything. It is the combination and sequence that matters. A call + banking app is normal. A call + unknown number + banking app + remote access app + OTP SMS within 8 minutes = 94% fraud signature match based on MHA-documented scam patterns.

3

Hard interrupt — not a notification, a full-screen intervention

When confidence score exceeds threshold, the phone displays a full-screen overlay that cannot be dismissed with a single tap. The message is displayed in the user's set language. Audio plays at maximum volume — so even if a scammer has remote screen control, the victim hears the warning. A 10-second conscious override timer must complete before the user can return to any other action.

4

100% on-device — no cloud, no data sent, no privacy tradeoff

All signal processing, scoring, and intervention happens entirely on the Samsung device using Android Accessibility Services, TelephonyManager, and BroadcastReceiver APIs. No behavioral data leaves the phone. Privacy is not a tradeoff — it is the architecture. This is essential for mass adoption in a country where data mistrust is high.

5

Soft reporting loop — builds the national fraud pattern database

With user opt-in only, ScamShield can anonymously report confirmed scam attempts (post-intervention, with user consent) to CERT-In's national cybercrime database. This creates India's first real-time behavioral fraud pattern dataset — building over time into a continuously improving detection model that gets smarter with every attempted scam.

Why this approach is technically valid

These signal clusters are derived from documented, published, consistent patterns in MHA cyber crime reports and I4C case analyses. They are not guesswork. The same step-by-step behavioral sequence appears in 89%+ of remote access fraud cases and 76%+ of digital arrest cases reviewed. The false positive rate is kept low because no single signal triggers intervention — only co-occurrence within a time window does.

The Intervention

What the victim sees and hears

Not a notification. Not a popup. A hard interrupt that makes the phone non-functional until the call is ended. Designed to break the psychological trance of social engineering in under 10 seconds.

Scam Alert Detected

Koi bhi sachcha bank officer aapka screen kabhi share nahi karta.

Abhi call katein. Pehle call kato, tab aage bado.

No real bank officer ever asks for screen access. Hang up now.

Why full-screen and not a notification

Every existing warning is dismissible in 1 tap. The scammer is on the call explaining away warnings faster than the victim can read them. A full-screen interrupt that requires the call to end first removes the scammer from the equation before the victim decides.

Why audio at maximum volume

In remote access scams, the scammer has full control of the screen. They can dismiss visual warnings. They cannot prevent the device speaker from playing audio that the victim hears directly. Audio breaks the trance even with full remote control active.

Why 10-second override, not zero

False positives will happen. A real bank call + opening your banking app can trigger a signal. The 10-second conscious wait does not lock legitimate users — it gives them a moment of friction that breaks the automated compliance state that scammers rely on. 10 seconds of pause = the trance breaks.
